Population Vulnerabilities

Non-White Non-Hispanic: Share of the population that is non-white.
Low-income population: Share of population with income less than 200% of the federal poverty line.
Limited English Proficiency: Share of the population 5 years of age and older that speaks a language other than English at home and speaks English less than "very well".
Severely Rent Burdened: Share of households that are spending more than 50% of their income on rent compared to the citywide average.

Housing Conditions

Rental Units: Share of occupied housing units that are rented.
Housing That Is Not Income-restricted: Share of all area units that do not include affordable units created or preserved since 2014 and are not owned by the New York City Housing Authority.
Housing That Is Not Rent-stabilized: Share of units that are not rent stabilized​ compared to the citywide average.
S3+ Maintenance Deficiencies: Share of units with three or more maintenance deficiencies compared to the citywide average.

Market Pressures

Change In Rents: Change between 2006-10 and 2015-19 ACS in median gross rents inflated to 2019 dollars compared to the citywide average.
Housing Price Appreciation: Increase in value of sales prices between 2000 and 2020 for 1-4 unit homes, 5+ unit rentals, coops, condos, and small mixed-use properties. A value of 2 means that prices are 2 times higher in 2020 than they were in 2000.
Change In Bachelor's Degree: Change between 2006-10 and 2015-19 ACS in the share of the population between the ages of 25 and 64 with a bachelor's degree or higher compared to the citywide average.
Adjacent Neighborhood Pressure: Areas adjacent to neighborhoods with high Market Pressure per the other data points in this category.
