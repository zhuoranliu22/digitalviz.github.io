Demographic Analysis

  1. Home
  2. Demographic Analysis

The infrastructure of the community could be improved through the gentrification process, residents with higher income would be attracted into the community, which can increase median house income within the community. However, gentrification can also lead to the displacement of low-income residents, who have to revert into affordable housing options within the community, resulting in a decrease in house incomes. In short, gentrification widens income inequality within communities.

The displacement of low-income residents results in an increase in poverty rates and a corresponding reduction in accessing basic community services and resources. However, the investment at the same time could be attracted by gentrification, which may increase employment opportunities and reduce poverty rates to some extent.

As a result of the global economic recession, most boroughs witnessed a reduction in median house income and an increase in poverty rates between 2006 and 2010. Overall, Manhattan's house income topped the list of the five boroughs in 2019, the poverty rate of which was steadily decreased, suggesting that this borough experienced further gentrification and brought economic growth.
