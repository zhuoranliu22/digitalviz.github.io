Housing Market Analysis

  1. Home
  2. Housing Market Analysis

As wealthier residents move into gentrifying communities, the number of houses sold in the community often tends to increase. The infrastructure in these communities undergoing gentrification is improved, which attracts investors to purchase properties in the seek of potential gains. This also increases investment by property developers, promoting the renovation of existing properties and the construction of new ones. An overall increase in sales volume could be seen in all five boroughs between 2010 and 2021, with Queens and Brooklyn showing the most significant rise.

Gentrification also tends to increase sales prices. As can be seen from the chart, sales medium price per unit increased in all five boroughs between 2006 and 2020, particularly for 1 family building types. However, in 2021, sales median price in Manhattan witnessed a significant decline. The increase in sales median price comes from gentrification, which promotes refurbishment of the houses and to some extent leads to a decline in the supply of affordable housing and a consequent rise in prices. The renovation of houses includes the upgrading of properties and the high-end amenities introduction, which increases the value of properties and sales prices. Hence, wealthier residents would be willing to pay a premium.

The increase in the sales volume and sales median price of housing market in New York City has led to a risk of displacement in the city. As wealthier residents tend to move into working class communities, lower income residents would be more likely to be squeezed out. The rising costs of housing costs would exacerbate the displacement problem. In general, Manhattan has largely completed the process of gentrification; conversely, Queens and Brooklyn residents are at higher risk of displacement. These two boroughs face higher levels of economic and social inequality.
